Description

The Geological Sample Material Type vocabulary is a SKOS-based hierarchical classification system primarily for classifying materials in earth sciences collections. The scheme provides comprehensive coverage of the earth sciences domain by accommodating both natural and processed materials, terrestrial and extraterrestrial sources, and the practical needs of collection management across diverse geological institutions.

Astromaterial

Description A geologic material that is extraterrestrial in origin or derived from a geological process involving an extraterrestrial material (impactite)

Fossil

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/fossil
Description Any remains, trace, or imprint of a plant or animal preserved in the Earth's crust since some past geologic or prehistoric time; loosely, any evidence of past life.
Is Defined By This vocabulary
Has Narrower Trace Fossil

Gemstone

IRI https://linked.data.gov.au/def/geological-sample-type/gemstone
Description Any mineral or naturally occurring substance that, when cut and polished, has sufficient beauty, rarity, and durability for use as a personal adornment or other ornament. Organic materials such as pearl are considered gem materials rather than gemstones. Because of their desirability and demand, synthetic and imitation gemstones are common replacements for natural gemstones.
Is Defined By Geological Sample Type vocabulary
Has Broader Mineral
Alternative Label Gem

Geological Material

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/geological-material
Description A material entity comprised of a natural amount of matter generated by some geologic process, includes synthetic material intended to represent a geological material
Is Defined By This vocabulary
Has Narrower Unconsolidated Material
Alternative Label Material Entity Type

Meteorite

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/meteorite
Description A natural, solid object, derived from a celestial body, that was transported by natural means from the body on which it formed to a region outside the dominant gravitational influence of that body and that later collided with a natural or artificial body larger than itself.
Is Defined By This vocabulary
Has Broader Astromaterial

Mineral

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/mineral
Description A naturally occurring element or chemical compound of homogeneous composition, normally with a crystalline structure, and resulting from some geological process on Earth or in extraterrestrial bodies
Is Defined By This vocabulary
Has Broader Geological Material
Has Narrower Synthetic Mineral

Ore

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/ore
Description A naturally occurring geological material from which economically valuable minerals can be extracted.
Is Defined By This vocabulary
Has Broader Geological Material

Rock

IRI https://w3id.org/gso/1.0/rockmaterial/Rock
Description Consolidated aggregate of one or more EarthMaterials, or a body of undifferentiated mineral matter, or of solid organic material. Includes mineral aggregates such as granite, shale, marble; glassy matter such as obsidian; and organic material such a coal. Excludes unconsolidated materials.
Is Defined By Rock Material Ontology
Has Broader Geological Material
Has Narrower Rock Core

Rock Core

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/rock-core
Description A geological material, commonly cylindrical in shape, recovered from a borehole through drilling
Is Defined By This vocabulary
Has Broader Rock

Sample Return

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/sample-return
Description Astromaterial returned from sample collection missions to celestial objects (e.g. comets, asteroids, the Moon, Mars)
Is Defined By This vocabulary
Has Broader Astromaterial

Synthetic Mineral

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/synthetic-mineral

Trace Fossil

IRI https://rs.tdwg.org/vocabularies/geological-specimen-material-types/trace-fossil

Is Defined By This vocabulary

Unconsolidated Material

IRI https://w3id.org/gso/1.0/rockmaterial/Unconsolidated_Material
